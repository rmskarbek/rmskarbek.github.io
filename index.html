Associate Research Scientist, LDEO

Currently a Postdoctoral Research Scientist at the Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory , I recieved my Ph.D. in 2015 from the University of Oregon, under the supervision of Alan Rempel. At LDEO, I work in the Rock and Ice Mechanics Lab on a variety of projects. My research methods focus on a combination of numerical/theoretical techniques with experimental work.

Publication

  • Deformation induced by fluid-rock hydration and carbonation reactions. (Skarbek et al., 2018)
  • Coulomb wedge models of deformation in accretionary prisms with heterogeneous mechanical properties. (Skarbek and Rempel, 2017)
  • Frictional and hydrogeologic properties of slow slip events in subduction zones. (Skarbek et al. 2012; Skarbek and Rempel, 2016)
